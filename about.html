Passion, Creativity Javascript, Python, Ruby, React, Bootstrap, jQuery, Django, Rails, PostgreSQL, MongoDB, Express.js,

I began my journey as a software developer from a chef. I spent most my working life in the kitchen, building teams, creating menus, living a very fast paced enviornmnet. Unfortunately, I was in a motorcycle accident and was left with this uncertainty about my carreer, greatfully it led me to discover my passion for the software industry. I was yearning for something to expand my creativity, I knew I could find something out there where I could create and enjoy like I once did. I began to discover online resources and advertisements offering accelerated programs to build a new carrer. I was definitely intrigued by these, I decided to dig in deeper. I was taking every course I could get my hands on, including resources such as; freecodecamp.org, codecademy.com, sololearn.com, udemy.com, and many tutorials along the way. Pursureing these, I was able to get familiar with HTML, CSS, Bootstrap, Javascript, jQuery, PHP, C++, C#, Ruby, SQL, TypeScript, CoffeeScript, Git, GitHub-Pages, bash, zsh. Now it was time to learn more.

A few months later I decided that software developing is what I want to do with the rest of my life. I started to reach out to individuals in my community and started getting a conversation going. I found that a lot of people in the industry were fond of these bootcamps where you can enhance yours skills with a 500-hour intense training camp, so I decided to pursure that next. I started doing some research and started visiting thsese schools to get a better feel of how it would be like to be in this industry. Once I found my match I knew I was going to build something great. Throughout the program I was assigned 3 - 5 assignments a day that ranged from a recap exercise from the content of the day prior, to the brand new content we learned that day. I started to develop my skills very fast now, I was getting better each day. I began to expand my skills in; Python, Django, React, SQL, and JEST. As days came closer to our final project day I was excited to showcase what we learned. I was able to get a full-stack project up with the short 9 days we had to complete it. I used a React, Django, PostgreSQL stack. The web-applications' intention was to bring communities together by the service or lending items or services to each other. I developed a borrowing system where users can search for an item and are shown a list of profiles with that item available. Then the user can choose to message the user through a custom messaging system, and request to borrow the item. Furthermore, I integrated google maps api to show the users position on a map and the users around who are signed up will display on the map accordingly. There were many challenges I faced when building this, one of which being the map system I initially had went down the night before presentation so rebuilding the map with a very short unexpected deadline was a great experience I now know to prepare and learn from. Another great learning experience was, the realization of the importance of planning. While starting the web-app, I was not familiar with react as it was the last thing we learned in the program. I took on the challenge of learning and planning at the same time. I was very confused and I must admit began to get frustrated with a whole new approach using react state management. I took the effort to understand the documentation, to learn how the framework works and to practice with endless failures. But with all failures, comes progress, lessons, and motivation. I did not give up when I had tried to add authentication to the project and I spent 3 days looking for a space that was being recognized as an indent in python. The solution ended up being to copy and paste two identical code snippets. And all I could do was laugh.

The frustrations of small simple mistakes can be devasting and make you want to quit, but I push forward as hard as I can, and I know that there will be a solution to every problem.
